How do we use your Google account?

We ask for what appears as three permissions View and manage Google Drive files that you have opened or created with this app, View your email, and Know who you are on Google. These permissions let us create files in your Drive, and update those files later. Also, if in the future we provide a way for you to directly upload Pyret files and load them into code.pyret.org from your Drive, you can explicitly grant permission for this application to view and edit certain files. We need to know your identity because this is the only way we've found to reliably give you persistent access to saving to Drive that doesn't expire and require popping up new windows while you're editing.

You can always go to https://security.google.com/settings/security/permissions?pli=1 and immediately remove all access that we have to your account. Your programs and code.pyret.org folder will not be deleted if you do so, but you can always manually delete the folder and its contents yourself.

Where are things stored in my Drive?

We initially make a single folder in your Google Drive, called code.pyret.org, and store all of your programs there, with the names you choose for them. They are all created with you as the owner, and private to your account.

If you share programs, we create a new folder, called code.pyret.org.shared, and we make a publicly-readable copy of any programs you share in that directory. If you delete this files in this folder, links for those programs you have shared with others will stop working, but any copies others have made will be theirs to keep. Each time you share a program, a new copy is created from its current contents.

We can only see files that we created, so simply moving another file into your code.pyret.org collection won’t cause it to show up in our interface: you need to create the file manually through our editor.

The listing of programs in this application only looks for the code.pyret.org folder and its contents, so if you rename or remove that folder, a new, empty one will be created for you on your next login.

What information is sent back to the Pyret team?

By default, Pyret records basic information about your use of code.pyret.org (CPO) to give us the background we need to improve the language. As you use use CPO, we take note of whether you are running with the type checker or not, and what errors you encounter.

Under the menu, you can opt-in to contributing more detailed information. If you choose to contribute detailed information, CPO will additionally send us data about what kind of edits you make to your program. We analyze this information to help us understand whether the error messages you recieve succeed in helping you reach a solution.
